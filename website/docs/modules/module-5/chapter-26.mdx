## Lesson: Social Cognition and Emotional Intelligence in Humanoid Robots

### Introduction to Social Cognition in Robots

Social cognition in humanoid robots refers to the ability of artificial systems to perceive, interpret, and respond to social cues and contexts in human-like environments. This capability is essential for humanoid robots to function effectively in human-centric spaces, requiring sophisticated understanding of social norms, emotional states, and interpersonal dynamics.

The components of social cognition in humanoid robots include:
- **Social Perception**: Recognition and interpretation of social signals
- **Social Understanding**: Comprehension of social contexts and relationships
- **Social Reasoning**: Decision-making based on social information
- **Social Learning**: Acquisition of social behaviors and norms
- **Emotional Processing**: Recognition, understanding, and expression of emotions
- **Theory of Mind**: Understanding others' mental states and beliefs
