## Lesson: Power Systems and Energy Management for Humanoid Robots

### Introduction to Power Systems

Power systems in humanoid robots are critical for enabling extended operation in human environments. Unlike stationary robots that can be continuously connected to power sources, humanoid robots must carry their energy storage systems, making energy efficiency a fundamental design constraint. The power system must provide adequate power for all robot subsystems while maintaining safe operation and reasonable operating duration.

Key requirements for humanoid robot power systems include:
- **Energy Density**: High capacity in minimal weight and volume
- **Power Delivery**: Adequate instantaneous power for peak demands
- **Efficiency**: Minimal energy loss during conversion and distribution
- **Safety**: Protection against thermal and electrical hazards
- **Reliability**: Consistent performance over extended periods
- **Cost**: Affordable solutions for practical deployment

### Battery Technologies

**Lithium-Ion Batteries**:
- Highest energy density among practical battery technologies
- Applications to most humanoid robot platforms
- Voltage stability during discharge
- Charging and maintenance requirements
- Safety considerations (thermal runaway risks)

**Lithium-Polymer Batteries**:
- Flexible form factor for integration
- Similar energy density to Li-ion
- Applications to conformal battery designs
- Manufacturing cost considerations
- Safety management requirements

**Nickel Metal Hydride Batteries**:
- Lower energy density but proven reliability
- Applications in safety-critical systems
- Temperature performance characteristics
- Environmental impact considerations
- Cost and replacement factors

**Advanced Battery Technologies**:

- Integration challenges
