## Lesson: Safety Systems and Fail-Safe Mechanisms in Humanoid Robotics

### Introduction to Safety in Humanoid Robotics

Safety systems in humanoid robotics are critical for protecting humans, property, and the robot itself during operation. Unlike industrial robots that operate in controlled environments with physical barriers, humanoid robots work in close proximity to humans in unpredictable environments. This requires comprehensive safety systems that can prevent, detect, and respond to potential hazards in real-time.

The key safety principles in humanoid robotics include:
- **Intrinsic Safety**: Design features that inherently prevent hazards
- **Functional Safety**: Active systems that detect and respond to hazards
- **Fail-Safe Operation**: Mechanisms that ensure safe states during failures
- **Human-Centric Safety**: Prioritization of human safety above all else
- **Redundancy**: Multiple independent safety systems
- **Predictability**: Consistent and predictable safety behavior
