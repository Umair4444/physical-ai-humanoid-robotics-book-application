## Lesson: Whole-Body Motion Control and Coordination

### Introduction to Whole-Body Motion Control

Whole-body motion control represents the integration of all degrees of freedom in humanoid robots to achieve coordinated, stable, and efficient movement patterns. Unlike controlling individual limbs or subsystems separately, whole-body control considers the robot as a coupled multi-body system where all components must work together harmoniously to accomplish tasks while maintaining balance and safety.

The fundamental challenges in whole-body motion control include:
- **Multi-Task Coordination**: Managing simultaneous and potentially conflicting objectives
- **Dynamic Balance**: Maintaining stability during complex movements
- **Computational Complexity**: Solving optimization problems in real-time
- **Constraint Handling**: Managing joint limits, contacts, and environmental constraints
- **Real-Time Performance**: Computing solutions within tight timing constraints
- **Safeguarding**: Ensuring safe interaction with humans and environment
