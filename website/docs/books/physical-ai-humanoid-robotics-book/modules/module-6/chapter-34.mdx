## Lesson: Manipulation and Grasping Control in Humanoid Robots

### Introduction to Manipulation and Grasping Control

Manipulation and grasping control in humanoid robots encompasses the complex systems required for dexterous interaction with objects in the environment. This capability is fundamental for humanoid robots to perform meaningful tasks in human environments, requiring sophisticated integration of perception, planning, control, and learning systems.

The core challenges in manipulation and grasping control include:
- **Dexterity**: Achieving human-like fine manipulation skills
- **Stability**: Maintaining balance during manipulation tasks
- **Adaptability**: Handling unknown objects and changing conditions
- **Safety**: Ensuring safe interaction with humans and objects
- **Real-Time Performance**: Responding quickly to dynamic situations
- **Multi-Task Coordination**: Managing manipulation while performing other tasks
