## Lesson: Footstep Planning and Path Generation for Humanoid Navigation

### Introduction to Footstep Planning

Footstep planning for humanoid navigation is a critical component that bridges high-level path planning with low-level locomotion control. It involves determining where and when to place footsteps to achieve stable, efficient, and safe locomotion through complex environments while maintaining dynamic balance and avoiding obstacles.

The fundamental challenges in footstep planning include:
- **Stability**: Ensuring each footstep placement contributes to overall balance
- **Feasibility**: Making sure planned steps are achievable by the robot
- **Optimality**: Optimizing for various criteria like energy, time, or comfort
- **Real-Time Performance**: Computing plans quickly enough for dynamic navigation
- **Uncertainty Handling**: Dealing with sensor noise and environmental uncertainties
- **Terrain Adaptation**: Adapting to various ground types and obstacles
