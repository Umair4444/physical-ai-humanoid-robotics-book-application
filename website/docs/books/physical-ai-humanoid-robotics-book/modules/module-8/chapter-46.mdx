## Lesson: Cognitive Architectures and Planning Systems in Physical AI

### Introduction to Cognitive Architectures in Physical AI

Cognitive architectures in Physical AI represent integrated systems of computational mechanisms that enable robots to perform cognitive tasks normally associated with minds: perception, reasoning, learning, planning, decision-making, and natural interaction. These architectures must handle the unique challenges of embodied intelligence, where cognition must be grounded in physical interaction with the real world.

Key challenges for cognitive architectures in Physical AI include:
- **Embodied Cognition**: Understanding how thinking emerges from the body and its interactions
- **Real-Time Processing**: Maintaining responsive behavior in dynamic environments
- **Multi-Modal Integration**: Coordinating diverse sensory and motor modalities
- **Uncertainty Management**: Reasoning with incomplete or noisy information
- **Learning and Adaptation**: Updating cognitive models through experience
- **Social Interaction**: Understanding and responding to human cognitive patterns
- **Scalability**: Managing increasing complexity of cognitive functions
