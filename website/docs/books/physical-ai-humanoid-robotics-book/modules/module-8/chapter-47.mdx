## Lesson: Learning from Demonstration and Transfer Learning

### Introduction to Learning from Demonstration

Learning from Demonstration (LfD) is a paradigm in robotics and AI where systems acquire new skills and behaviors by observing and imitating human demonstrations or expert behaviors. This approach is particularly valuable for humanoid robots, as it allows for the transfer of human-like skills and behaviors that would otherwise require extensive programming or time-consuming reinforcement learning.

The core principles of Learning from Demonstration include:
- **Behavior Transfer**: Moving human skills to robot platforms
- **Generalization**: Applying learned behaviors to new situations
- **Adaptation**: Adjusting demonstrations to robot capabilities
- **Efficiency**: Rapid skill acquisition compared to trial-and-error learning
- **Naturalness**: Acquiring human-like interaction patterns
- **Safety**: Learning safe behaviors from expert demonstrations
