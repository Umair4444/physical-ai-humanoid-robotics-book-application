## Lesson: Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) for Humanoid Robots

### Introduction to SLAM in Humanoid Robotics

Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) represents one of the most critical capabilities for humanoid robots operating in unknown or dynamically changing environments. Unlike wheeled robots that primarily navigate on flat surfaces, humanoid robots require SLAM systems that can handle complex 3D environments, varying terrains, and the dynamic nature of human environments while maintaining the robot's balance and ensuring safe interaction with humans.

The core challenges of SLAM for humanoid robots include:
- **3D Environment Mapping**: Creating comprehensive 3D models of complex indoor and outdoor spaces
- **Dynamic Balance Integration**: Maintaining balance while performing SLAM operations
- **Real-Time Performance**: Operating SLAM algorithms within tight timing constraints
- **Human Safety**: Ensuring safe navigation in human-populated environments
- **Computational Efficiency**: Running complex algorithms on robot hardware
- **Robustness**: Handling sensor failures and environmental changes
