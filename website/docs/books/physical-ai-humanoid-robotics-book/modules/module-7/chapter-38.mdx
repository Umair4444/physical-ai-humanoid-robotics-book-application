## Lesson: 3D Perception and Spatial Understanding for Humanoids

### Introduction to 3D Perception in Humanoids

3D perception and spatial understanding form the foundation for humanoid robots to navigate, interact with objects, and operate effectively in three-dimensional environments. Unlike 2D perception which provides limited spatial information, 3D perception enables robots to understand depth, volume, spatial relationships, and geometric properties of objects and environments, which is essential for human-like manipulation, navigation, and interaction.

The core requirements for 3D perception in humanoid robots include:
- **Spatial Mapping**: Creating accurate 3D representations of environments
- **Object Understanding**: Recognizing and manipulating 3D objects
- **Navigation Support**: Enabling safe and efficient 3D navigation
- **Real-Time Processing**: Operating with fast 3D data processing
- **Robustness**: Handling varying lighting and environmental conditions
- **Integration**: Combining multiple 3D sensing modalities
