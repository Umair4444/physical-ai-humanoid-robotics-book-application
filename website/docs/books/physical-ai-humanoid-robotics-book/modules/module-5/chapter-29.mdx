## Lesson: Trust and Safety in Human-Robot Interactions

### Introduction to Trust and Safety in HRI

Trust and safety are fundamental pillars of successful human-robot interaction, particularly crucial in humanoid robotics where robots operate in close proximity to humans in unstructured environments. Trust refers to the confidence humans have in a robot's capabilities, reliability, and intentions, while safety encompasses the physical, psychological, and ethical protection of human users during interaction.

The interdependence of trust and safety in HRI includes:
- **Physical Safety**: Preventing harm to humans during interaction
- **Psychological Safety**: Ensuring comfortable and non-threatening interaction
- **Trust Building**: Establishing and maintaining user confidence
- **Transparency**: Clear communication of robot capabilities and limitations
- **Predictability**: Consistent and reliable robot behavior
- **Ethical Considerations**: Ensuring responsible interaction practices
